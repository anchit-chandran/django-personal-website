About Me

I'm an ex-doctor who graduated from Imperial College School of Medicine and Imperial Business School.

Currently, I'm freelancing as a clinical software developer, focussing on Python projects to improve healthcare.

I entered Medicine to realise positive change on a societal level. However, working in the NHS, I experienced too many barriers and inefficiencies preventing large-scale sustainable change. So instead, I'm turning to an outside-in approach through a pivot into healthcare software development - skipping the red tape and bureaucracy - and democratising the future of healthcare.

My favourite language is Python, which I learned through self-teaching from online resources. I founded my side hustle, MediCode, when realising there were no programming tutorials catered to medics. I also have experience with languages such as HTML/CSS/JavaScript, R, and SQL. I hold an internship with Great Ormond Street Hospital's data science team, DRIVE, and my freelancing includes clients such as the RCPCH.

My main interests are data science and machine learning. My career aspirations involve applying computational methods to understand the mind, improve mental health, and explore consciousness.
